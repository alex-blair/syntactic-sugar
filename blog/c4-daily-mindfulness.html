A 'mindfulness conversation'

This week we were asked to try a 'mindfulness conversation', which involves listening to another person for 4 minutes without interrupting, then repeating back what you think you heard (called "looping").

Sustaining mindfulness

There are lots of things I tell myself I should do every day, like exercising for at least 30 minutes or fitting in 10,000 steps. I know they'll be good for me. I want to make time for them. But sometimes I just can't be bothered. I need to get better at developing goals into habits.

Search Inside Yourself suggests three techniques for sustaining mindfulness practice:

  • Find a buddy to practice with you and hold you to account.
  • Do less than you can, so that it feels like an indulgence rather than a chore.
  • Take one mindful breath a day. Everything else after that is a bonus.
My favourite is number two. I like the idea of fitting in a few minutes here and there. I think it also makes sense to build mindfulness into things I'm already doing, like eating, walking, and talking to others, because then I'll be prompted to practice it throughout the day. I came across this quote, which I think sums up my ideal practice quite well: "Enjoy simple things with total intensity. Just a cup of tea can be a deep meditation." (~ Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh).
