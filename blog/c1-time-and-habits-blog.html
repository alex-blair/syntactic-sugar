Timekeeping and Habits

The Pomodoro Technique

  • Work for 25 minutes then take a short break.
  • After 4 pomodoros, take a longer break.
  • Thoughts? I've found that short breaks tend to spiral into long breaks when I don't carefully plan all the tasks I want to get through in a few hours.
  • Advantages: a sense of satisfaction as I checked things off my to-do list, and was able to really enjoy the breaks without feeling guilty.

Growth Mindset vs Fixed Mindset

  • Fixed mindset - believing that your talent is fixed, and that if you don't have the right level of natural talent, you won't be able to do something.
  • Growth mindset - believing that your talents can be developed through effort, good teaching and persistence.
  • To move from a fixed to a growth mindset - start small. Prove your ability to yourself in a thousand tiny ways, and let your confidence grow naturally from there.
  • Advantages of a growth mindset: there's always room for improvement. You can get better if you work at it.

Experimental Study Habits

  • Start small. The hardest part is getting started, so start really small and build momentum from there.
  • "Work expands to fill the time available for its completion" - give yourself less time to do a task, and you'll get through it faster.
  • A little guilt can be a great motivator. Set deadlines that you can't ignore e.g. by promising someone else that you'll give them the work to review by a certain date.
