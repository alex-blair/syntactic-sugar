The three Ps of happiness

This week we're reading Chapter 6 of Search Inside Yourself, which looks at how to go about boosting motivation levels. The Chapter starts out with the basic idea that happiness is a key motivation for a lot of the things we do in life, so if you want to feel more motivated on a day-to-day basis you should try to understand the things that make you happy. Not only that, you should try to work out the things that make you sustainably happy (because chips might be great, but if you eat 3 bags in a row you probably won't feel as happy at the end as you did when you started). The Chapter groups happiness into three different forms:

  • Pleasure - a fleeting type of happiness that arises, say, from eating an extravagant meal or getting a present in the mail. Once the moment has passed or you get used to the situation, then you'll return to your default happiness level.
  • Passion - "where peak performance meets peak engagement" (p 112). Those moments when you're fully immersed in a task that's both challenging and interesting, and you wonder how it suddenly became night-time.
  • Purpose - when you're doing something you believe is important and which has meaning to you.
