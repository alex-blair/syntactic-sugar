Trust

Chapter 7 of Search Inside Yourself looks at building trust. We've been asked to think of a time when we tried to grow trust, and how it went. I tried this recently with a friend who I didn't think could keep a secret. Little by little I started telling her more things, letting her know which things were private and which were public, and seeing how it went. It seemed counter-intuitive at first - sharing more information in the hopes that she would share less. But it helped.

The other side of the story

  1. Think of a difficult situation from your past, when there was some conflict or disagreement.
  2. Describe the situation as though you are 100 percent correct and reasonable.
  3. Now describe the situation as though the other person is 100 percent correct and reasonable.

A while ago I bought a compost bin for our flat. I wanted us to put all our kitchen scraps in it so we could reduce waste and I could use compost in the garden. The neighbour thought this was a bad idea. I'll try to look at the situation through from his perspective:

  • our houses are very close together;
  • compost bins can attract pests like flies and rats;
  • it would suck if flies or rats came onto my property because someone was feeding them and helping them breed with kitchen scraps.
As a note, I didn't get rid of the compost bin, but I've become a lot more selective about the sorts of things I put in it. Because yea, pests aren't great.
